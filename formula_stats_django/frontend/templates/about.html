Our Mission

The Story of Speed, Told Through Data

Formula Stats was born from a passion for motorsport and a love for data. Our goal is to make Formula 1 analysis simple, accessible, and insightful for every fan, analyst, and enthusiast. We provide the tools to look beyond the broadcast and uncover the strategic depth of every race.

Behind the Project

An Open-Source Analytics Platform

This project was created by Bartosz Tylczy≈Ñski, a Computer Science student at the University of London, to bridge the gap between raw F1 data and clear, actionable insights. Formula Stats is a Django-based web application focused on providing clean race visuals using detailed telemetry and lap data, powered by a comprehensive PostgreSQL database.

It's built to be lightweight, fast, and entirely community-driven. Whether you're comparing driver performance, dissecting tyre strategies, or just curious about the data, this platform is for you.

Intuitive Visualisations
Explore detailed plots of lap times, telemetry, tyre strategies, and more for any race weekend.
Powerful Filtering
Select any season, event, or session to tailor your data exploration and focus on what matters most to you.
Downloadable Graphics
Export high-quality images of all charts and graphics for easy sharing or for use in your own analysis.
Open Source
The entire project is open source. Contributions are welcome to expand features, fix bugs, and improve documentation.
